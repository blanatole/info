I am an Information Technology researcher with research interests in artificial intelligence, natural language processing, knowledge graphs, knowledge graph completion, fact verification, and trustworthy machine learning. My work focuses on designing reproducible and auditable AI methods, especially for evidence organization, graph-based reasoning, and evaluation protocols for knowledge-intensive tasks.

I am particularly interested in knowledge graph reasoning, provenance-aware fact verification, retrieval-augmented verification, and robust evaluation of AI systems under sparse or noisy evidence conditions. My research aims to develop methods that are not only effective in predictive performance but also transparent, explainable, and reproducible for academic and practical use.

My current research directions include knowledge graph completion, graph-based evidence modeling, fact-checking systems, and evaluation frameworks for reliable AI.
